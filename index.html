The Struggle of Every Candidate

Winning an election in India shouldn't be a battle against paperwork. Legacy systems are holding back the future of governance.

"I spent 14 hours a day just trying to find one name. The data was there, but it was trapped in dead paper." — Aman, Independent Candidate (UP)
Aman's Story

Aman, an independent candidate from a bustling constituency in Uttar Pradesh, found his campaign stalled before it even began. Every morning, his team faced a mountain of over 20,000 pages of PDF voter lists.

They spent weeks manually highlighting names, trying to sort voters by street, and guessing caste demographics. The "voter list" wasn't a resource — it was a prison. While his opponents used expensive consultancies, Aman had nothing.
